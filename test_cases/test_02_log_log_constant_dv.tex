% Test Case 02: Log-Log Demand Constant Elasticity
% Difficulty: Medium

\begin{claim}
For a log-linear demand function $q(p) = A \cdot p^{-\varepsilon}$ where $A > 0$,
$\varepsilon > 0$, and $p > 0$, the price elasticity of demand is constant and equal
to $-\varepsilon$. Formally:
\[
  \frac{dq}{dp} \cdot \frac{p}{q} = -\varepsilon
\]
\end{claim}
